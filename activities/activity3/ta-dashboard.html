AI-Generated Leading Indicator Weights

In this activity, students create AI prompts to generate optimal weights for leading economic indicators. As a TA, you can collect these AI responses and analyze how they compare to student-created indices.

How It Works

  1. Students create AI prompts: In step 3 of the index-builder activity, students download Z-score data and use a template prompt to ask AI assistants for optimal indicator weights.
  2. Collect AI responses: Have students submit their AI-generated weights via your preferred method (email, Canvas, etc.).
  3. Compile responses: Create an Excel or CSV file with the AI-generated weights. Each row should represent one AI model's weights for all indicators.
  4. Upload and analyze: Use the AI Weights Analysis tool to compare AI-generated weights with class average weights.

File Format

Create an Excel (.xlsx) or CSV file with the following format, or download our template. New! We now support multiple AI models comparison in one view.

Legacy Template New Multi-Model Template

Legacy Format (still supported):

model_name 10Y2Y_Yield ISM_NewOrders Building_Permits Consumer_Confidence PMI Initial_Claims Avg_WeeklyHours SP500
ChatGPT 20 15 10 15 10 15 5 10
Claude 25 15 10 10 10 20 5 5

New Format (recommended):

Indicator ChatGPT Claude Gemini Perplexity Mistral Deepseek
Yield Curve (10Y-2Y) 10 20 10 30 50 5
ISM New Orders 20 20 20 5 10 5
Building Permits 30 20 20 5 10 10

Important Format Requirements:

  • For legacy format: Column headers must match exactly as shown above
  • For new format: First column must be named "Indicator" and contain indicator names
  • Weights must be numbers that sum to 100 for each model
  • In legacy format: Each row represents one AI model's weights
  • In new format: Each column (except the first) represents one AI model's weights

Additional Notes:

  • The model name can be any text (e.g., "ChatGPT", "Claude", "Gemini")
  • You can include multiple AI models in one file
  • For best results, collect responses from different AI models
  • After uploading, you'll be able to compare AI weights with class average weights
  • The new format supports comparing weights from multiple AI models at once

This visualization shows how AI-generated weights typically compare to class average weights.

Key Differences

AI Models Typically:
  • Weight the Yield Curve (10Y-2Y) more heavily
  • Place less emphasis on PMI
  • Provide more balanced weights across indicators
  • Suggest lower thresholds for recession signals
Students Typically:
  • Weight Consumer Confidence more heavily
  • Place more emphasis on stock market performance
  • Have more variation in their weights
  • Set higher thresholds for recession signals

Teaching Tips

This activity helps students understand how AI can be used as a tool in economic analysis. Here are some tips for facilitating discussions:

Discussion Questions

  • How do AI-generated weights differ from student-created weights? Why might this be the case?
  • Which indicators did the AI models prioritize? Does this align with economic theory?
  • How did different AI models (e.g., ChatGPT vs. Claude) differ in their approaches?
  • Did the AI models perform better or worse than student-created indices at predicting recessions?
  • What are the limitations of using AI for economic forecasting?

Consider having students present their AI-generated indices and explain the reasoning provided by the AI. This can lead to rich discussions about economic theory and the capabilities/limitations of AI in economic analysis.
